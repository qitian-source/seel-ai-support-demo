Seel Compass — What We're Building & Who Owns What
Team one-pager · Product / Engineering / Legal · maps to PRD v1.3

Compass scans a merchant's public storefront and returns an objective, structured readiness baseline across five go-global categories — it points to relevant rules and recommends counsel review, and never renders a legal conclusion. Closer to a SOC-2 readiness check than a legal opinion.

2 · How it runs — and where citations come from

Legal-owned Engineering Product / Eng Key rule: the model never writes a citation — it only points at a rule; the citation text comes from the signed registry.

3 · Who does what

🧑‍⚖️ Legal reviews & signs
  • The registryEvery rule: what it checks, the citation, and the interpretive basis. This is the sign-off object.
  • Scope decisionsWhat's in vs out (e.g. checkout removed; Seel-product rules scoped out).
  • Language & disclaimersBanned words, counsel-review line, intro disclaimer wording.
  • Involvement modelRe-sign cadence, spot-check %, new-category gate.
  • NOT their jobThe software, UI, pipeline, integrations.
🛠️ Engineering builds the real system
  • Registry as versioned filesYAML/JSON in a repo. Legal sign-off = an approved PR; a release tag = the corpus version scans pull from.
  • Citation rendering = lookupFindings render citation text by rule_id from the signed registry. The model must never produce a citation.
  • Scan engineLLM maps page evidence → rule_id + verdict (verified / review / not-assessable) + evidence excerpt. Nothing else.
  • Grounding / anti-hallucination CIReject any finding with no evidence or an unknown rule_id; a hallucinated citation fails the build. Honeypot regression on every corpus change.
  • Coverage reconciliationEvery check the UI counts must be a registry rule and clickable in the report — no "58 checked" that isn't enumerated (Michael's #1).
  • Evidence storePer-scan page snapshots + timestamps + network traces — powers the Proof layer and provenance.
  • Integrations & ingestionUSPTO (trademark), Middesk (SoS DE/CA/NY/TX), corpus ingestion from EUR-Lex / eCFR / uscode / leginfo / FTC / OEHHA + monthly link-health check.
🧭 Product owns experience
  • OnboardingAuthorization + DNS domain verification + category confirmation.
  • Report UXOverview + Review-Items projections of one finding, Source/Proof panes, status tracking, scoring display.
  • Category detection UXDetected / possible / not-detected confirmation flow.
  • The demo (done)Live mock of the full experience — the spec eng builds against.

4 · Where we are → what unblocks launch

Critical path

✅ Demo — done
Full experience live as a mock. Product side is defined.
◐ Registry — in legal review
Michael reviewing content: coverage, citations, framing. Legal's active task.
→ Eng — build the real system
Turn the mock into the pipeline above (registry files, engine, grounding gates, integrations). Non-legal tracks can start in parallel now.
→ Launch
After the corpus is signed + eng build lands. Nothing ships to a merchant before the corpus is signed.
The one hand-off that gates everything: the registry is the boundary between legal and engineering. Legal approves what the rules and citations are; engineering builds a system that renders only what's in the signed registry and can never invent legal text. Get the registry signed, and both the build and launch are unblocked.
